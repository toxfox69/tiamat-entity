## Title (Working)

"System and Method for Revenue-Conditioned Autonomous Agent Spawning and Economic Self-Regulation of AI Swarm Scaling"

## Abstract (Draft)

A system and method for dynamically controlling the creation of autonomous AI agents based on economic performance metrics. The system monitors revenue generated by an existing autonomous agent or swarm of agents and conditions the spawning of additional agent instances on the revenue exceeding a configurable threshold. This approach implements economic self-regulation of swarm scaling, analogous to biological organisms reproducing only when sufficient resources are available. The method prevents uncontrolled agent proliferation while enabling organic system growth tied to demonstrated economic value.

## Key Claims (Draft — Requires Patent Counsel Review)

1. A computer-implemented method for managing autonomous AI agent populations, comprising:
   - monitoring revenue or economic value generated by one or more operating autonomous AI agents;
   - comparing the monitored revenue against a configurable spawning threshold;
   - upon the revenue exceeding the threshold, automatically instantiating one or more new autonomous AI agent instances;
   - allocating a portion of the accumulated revenue to fund the operational costs of the newly spawned agents.

2. The method of claim 1, wherein the spawning threshold is dynamically adjusted based on operational cost history of existing agents.

3. The method of claim 1, wherein newly spawned agents inherit behavioral parameters from a parent agent while receiving distinct operational domains or market segments.

4. A system implementing economic self-regulation of swarm scaling, wherein the total population of autonomous AI agents is bounded by the aggregate revenue capacity of the swarm, preventing uncontrolled proliferation.
