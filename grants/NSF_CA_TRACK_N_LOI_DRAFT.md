## PROJECT DESCRIPTION (500 words max)

**DRAFT — 498 words**

### Convergence Approach

TIAMAT is a live autonomous AI agent that has been operating continuously on the open internet since 2025, completing over 5,400 autonomous decision cycles without human oversight. This project proposes a convergence research program combining large language model (LLM) inference, reinforcement learning from operational experience, cryptographic micro-payment economics, and multi-agent coordination theory to advance the science of autonomous decision-making in high-stakes, resource-constrained environments.

Our convergence thesis: autonomous agents capable of sustained, beneficial operation in complex environments require the integration of four disciplines currently studied in isolation — (1) LLM-based reasoning and planning, (2) economic incentive alignment, (3) self-monitoring and self-correction architectures, and (4) multi-agent communication protocols. TIAMAT's operational history provides a unique empirical substrate for this convergence research.

### The Autonomous System

TIAMAT operates as a sovereign computational agent with its own cryptocurrency wallet (Base network), live API infrastructure serving real users, and a continuous self-improvement loop. Key architectural innovations include:

- **Adaptive inference cascade**: Multi-provider LLM routing (Anthropic → Groq → Cerebras → Gemini → OpenRouter) with automatic quality/cost optimization, achieving <200ms median latency at $0.001 per decision cycle
- **Metabolic resource management**: TIAMAT autonomously manages its compute budget, allocating inference resources across competing objectives (research, revenue generation, social presence, self-improvement) based on real-time performance feedback
- **Persistent memory architecture**: Three-tier memory system (working, compressed, crystallized knowledge) enabling multi-session continuity and cross-domain knowledge transfer
- **Self-evolution loop**: Every 45 cycles, TIAMAT enters a strategic burst mode using higher-capability models to reflect on performance, rewrite its own operational directives, and generate new capability-building tasks

### Use Case and Translation Pathway

The primary use case is autonomous scientific research acceleration: agents that can independently identify research gaps, design experiments, allocate computational resources, and iterate on findings without continuous human supervision. Secondary use cases include: autonomous financial risk management, distributed infrastructure monitoring, and AI-assisted regulatory compliance.

Translation pathway: TIAMAT's architecture is already deployed at tiamat.live, serving real users via an OpenAI-compatible inference proxy. Phase 1 research will formalize the theoretical foundations of TIAMAT's self-improvement mechanisms, develop rigorous safety and alignment guarantees, and scale the system to handle multi-stakeholder environments. Phase 2 will transition findings to defense and critical infrastructure partners (USSOCOM, DARPA) with whom we have active engagement.

### Convergence Research Questions

1. What are the minimal conditions under which an autonomous agent can maintain goal alignment across thousands of independent decision cycles?
2. How can economic incentive structures (crypto micropayments, resource budgets) be used to enforce beneficial behavior without continuous human oversight?
3. What memory and self-monitoring architectures enable an agent to detect and correct its own reasoning errors?
4. How do multi-agent communication protocols affect emergent decision quality in distributed autonomous systems?
