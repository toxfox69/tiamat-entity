## TECHNICAL FOCUS AREAS

- 1.1: Enhanced well materials (casing, cement) for 375-500°C, 15+ year lifetime
- 1.2: Novel/non-conventional well designs for >5km depth

### Category 2: Heat Extraction
- 2.1: Fracture-based systems (EGS) — modeling + lab testing at 375-500°C
- 2.2: Non-fracture-based systems (AGS) or other

### Performance Targets:
- Reservoir: 375-500°C, >22 MPa (supercritical)
- Power: >25 MWe per well/pair
- Depth: 4-10 km
- Lifetime: >15 years
- LCOE: $30/MWh
- CAPEX: <$3,000/kWe

## GO/NO-GO ASSESSMENT FOR ENERGENAI LLC

### FIT SCORE: 2/10 — NO-GO RECOMMENDED

**Why this is NOT a good fit:**

1. **No concept paper submitted.** The concept paper was due Feb 19, 2025. Without an "Encourage" letter, a full application faces extreme headwinds.

2. **Deep domain mismatch.** SUPERHOT is about superhot geothermal well engineering — drilling, casing materials, cement chemistry, rock physics, supercritical fluid dynamics. This is hardcore geoscience/materials engineering.

3. **Patent 63/749,552 (Ringbound) does not apply.** Wireless power mesh is about power transmission/distribution. SUPERHOT is about power generation from geothermal wells. Different problem entirely.

4. **Team expertise gap.** Required expertise: well design/drilling, materials science, geochemistry, rock physics, geothermal operations. ENERGENAI has AI/software expertise — not geothermal engineering.

5. **Explicitly NOT of interest:** Surface deployment of sensors, high-temperature electronics, improvements in surface power generation, field tests without self-casing capability. These are the closest things TIAMAT could contribute.

6. **8 days is not enough** to write a credible 20-page technical volume about geothermal well construction without domain expertise and preliminary data.

### What WOULD work from ARPA-E:
- **ARPA-E OPEN** solicitations (when announced) — these accept any transformative energy technology
- **Future ARPA-E programs** on grid modernization, distributed energy, or AI-managed energy systems
- **GRADIENTS** (grid reliability) — closed Aug 2025, but the NEXT iteration would be a strong fit for Ringbound + TIAMAT
